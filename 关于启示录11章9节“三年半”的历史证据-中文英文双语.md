1793年10月5日【国民公会】颁布了十天为一周三十天为一个月的新历法，标志着法国与任何宗教的决裂。【2】<br>

On October 20, 1793, an even more severe law was enacted. This was an unjust measure directed against the clergy, for if as few as six citizens denounced a minister, he could be deported, and those with prior records could even be put to death.【3】<br>
1793年10月20日颁布了更加严厉的法律：这是针对神职人员的不公正法律，因为只要有六名公民举报一位神职人员，这位神职人员就会被驱逐，有案底的甚至会受到死刑。【3】<br>

1793年11月10日【国民公会】确立了理性崇拜，高举一位可耻的女人为理智的化身；当时“埃贝尔、肖梅特及其同伙出现在【国民公会】上，宣称‘上帝不存在，理性崇拜将代替上帝的地位。’”。【4】<br>

Then, on November 23, 1793, the Paris Commune, going beyond the National Convention, issued an order abolishing Christian worship and closing the churches. The two councils, namely the Council of Five Hundred (Conseil des Cinq-Cents) and the Council of Ancients (Conseil des Anciens), existed from September 27, 1795, to December 26, 1799. These bodies succeeded the National Convention on September 27, 1795.【6】<br>

我们需要注意在【五百人院】和【元老院】的期间1797年6月17日发出了真正地宗教自由的强烈呼吁。<br>

The historian Pierre de La Gorce particularly emphasized that the first truly effective appeal for religious liberty since the outbreak of the French Revolution was made by Camille Jordan on June 17, 1797. This appeal eventually led the French legislative bodies to restore religious liberty on August 24, 1797.<br>
历史学家拉戈尔斯Pierre de La Gorce 特别强调了法国大革命以来第一次真正发出有效的宗教自由呼吁是卡米尔·乔丹Camille Jordan在1797年6月17日。并且这次宗教自由的呼吁引发了法国的立法机构最终在1797年8月24日对宗教自由的恢复。<br>

Thus, while the French legislature, under the National Convention, did indeed abolish Christianity in November 1793, the later legislative bodies, namely the Council of Five Hundred and the Council of Ancients, through the repeal of the anti-clerical laws, restored genuine religious liberty between June 17 and August 24, 1797.【7】<br>
法国立法机构【国民公会】在1793年11月的确废除了基督教；然而，在1797年6月17日~8月24日底法国的立法机构【五百人院】和【元老院】通过废除针对神职人员的法律，恢复了真正的宗教自由。【7】<br>

Therefore, the legislative body that abolished Christianity and restored religious freedom was none other than the same French legislature, simply operating under different names at different times.<br>
因此，废除基督教和恢复宗教自由的机构都是法国的同一个立法机构只不过名字不一样。<br>

The National Convention (French: Convention nationale, [kɔ̃vɑ̃sjɔ̃ nɑsjɔnal]) was the constituent assembly of the Kingdom of France for one day and of the French First Republic for its first three years during the French Revolution, following the two-year National Constituent Assembly and the one-year Legislative Assembly. The National Convention was created after the insurrection of 10 August 1792. It was the first French government organized as a republic, abandoning the monarchy altogether. By creating an explicit executive body, this constitution concentrated power, but also limited how much any one individual or political faction could exert by sharing executive power among five Directors. Finally, the constitution proscribed political gatherings of any sort to prevent the re–formation of the club movement or the organization of national political parties.<br>
到1795年中期，民众对现状，尤其是政府的违宪性质，普遍感到不满。左翼人士要求“面包和1793年宪法”，而那些在恐怖统治下饱受苦难的人们则试图通过结束那些导致持续起义、各省内乱（特别是对恐怖统治时期当权者的报复）以及持续不断的海外战争（这些战争持续给国内经济带来沉重负担）的地区的民众政治活动来“结束革命”。为此，国民公会指派了一个包括西哀士在内的委员会起草另一部宪法，该宪法于8月22日提交。以下摘录表明，这部宪法力求确保革命以温和的方式延续，从而在个人自由（即个人财产权）的基础上，建立稳定的社会秩序，并兼顾司法平等，而非1793年宪法中所包含的直接民主和社会经济平等的保障。<mark>为了实现这种微妙的平衡，制宪者削弱了立法机构的权力，将立法机构改为两院制，使其无法迅速通过立法。</mark>通过设立明确的行政机构，这部宪法集中了权力，但同时也通过将行政权力分配给五位董事，限制了任何个人或政治派别所能施加的权力。最后，宪法禁止一切形式的政治集会，以防止俱乐部运动的复兴或全国性政党的组建。<br>

### 【7】The First Post-Revolutionary Appeal for Religious Liberty to Effect Concrete Change (June 17, 1797)<br>
### 【7】大革命后第一次引发实际改变的宗教自由之声1797年6月17日。<br>

Pierre de La Gorce, a French historian and magistrate, gave a particularly vivid account of this restoration of religious liberty in volume 4 of his History of the Religious Situation during the French Revolution. The following is a literal translation from the original French text (in English):<br>
法国的拉戈尔斯Pierre de La Gorce是历史学家也是法官，他在《法国大革命宗教史》卷四中特别描绘了这段恢复宗教自由的内容，以下是原文法语的直译英文：<br>

With impatience, people were waiting for the Commission to publish its views. On 29 Prairial (17 June 1797), the report was ready. I note this date of 29 Prairial. It deserves to be retained by all those who are interested in the history of the Church of France.<br>
人们焦急地等待着委员会公布其见解。共和历牧月29日（1797年6月17日），报告准备就绪。<br>
我记下了牧月29日这个日期。所有对法国教会历史感兴趣的人都应当记住它。<br>

Twice since the Terror, the cause of religious liberty had been solemnly pleaded. It had been pleaded by Bishop Grégoire before the Convention, on 1 Nivôse, Year III. It had been pleaded by Portalis before the assembly of the Ancients, on 9 Fructidor, Year IV. Of these two manifestos, the first had stirred the country more than the deputies. The second had checked one of the enterprises of persecution without striking down persecution itself. In this year 1797, everything was changed, and, for the first time since the Revolution, a voice capable of pronouncing true words of deliverance was about to resound in an assembly capable of understanding them.<br>
自大恐怖时期以来，宗教自由的事业曾两次得到庄严的申诉。第一次是格雷瓜尔主教于共和历3年雪月1日（1794年12月21日）在国民公会上提出的。第二次是波塔利斯于共和历4年果月9日（1796年8月26日）在元老院提出的。在这两份宣言中，第一份打动了国民，却甚于打动议员。第二份遏制了迫害行径的其中一次企图，却未能根除迫害本身。在1797这一年，一切都改变了，并且，自大革命以来第一次，一个能够发出真正解放之言的声音，将要在能够理解这些话语的议会中响起。<br>

The rapporteur chosen by the Commission was Camille Jordan.<br>
委员会选定的报告人是卡米尔·乔丹Camille Jordan。”<br>

——Pierre de La Gorce , Histoire religieuse de la Révolution français (Tome IV), 1921, Page 174<br>
——Pierre de La Gorce（皮埃尔·德·拉戈尔斯）Histoire religieuse de la Révolution français (Tome IV)《法国大革命宗教史》（第四卷）1921年，第174页<br>



Why did the historian La Gorce regard the appeal for religious liberty on June 17, 1797, as “the first voice... to utter true words of emancipation”? La Gorce himself explains this point in the subsequent discussion of volume 4 of his Histoire religieuse de la Révolution français, as follows:<br>
为什么历史学家拉戈尔斯认为1797年6月17日宗教自由呼吁是“第一次……发出真正解放之言的声音”呢？历史学家拉戈尔斯在《法国大革命宗教史》（第四卷）后续的内容中说明了这一点，内容如下：<br>

In the [APPENDIX 1 Chronology of the French Revolution] in William Doyle’s The Oxford History of the French Revolution, it is stated:<br>
在WILLIAM DOYLE的《牛津法国大革命史》的年表事件中写道：<br>

24 Aug. 	Laws against clergy repealed.<br>
1797年8月24日 废除针对神职人员的法律。<br>

Translator’s Note: The legislation repealed on August 24, 1797, had originally been promulgated on October 20, 1793.<br>
〈译者注：1797年8月24日废除的法律最初是在1793年10月20日颁布的。〉<br>

The Resolution of the Council of Five Hundred, concerning the deported priests, had been transmitted to the Ancients in order that they might convert it into law. On 7 Fructidor (24 August 1797), it came up on the order of the day. The reporter was Muraire, formerly one of the sponsors—though how disillusioned he was today—of the Civil Constitution of the Clergy. In a few brief words, he rose against the legislation concerning public worship: it had been severe at first, then inhuman, and had ended by becoming atrocious. Referring to the speech of Portalis and to the vote that had followed, he reminded his colleagues that, already in the previous year, they had protested against these severities. It was necessary now that an express repeal should erase the last traces of barbarous decrees. “Let priests,” said Muraire in closing, “be punished if they are guilty. But let persecution not be confused with justice.” When the reporter had finished, a few voices asked for postponement, but timidly, and without finding an echo; and immediately the Resolution was approved. One question still remained, that of the promise. In the Council of Five Hundred, the Commission charged with religious affairs was deliberating upon it. On 10 Fructidor, Dubruel, in the name of this commission, proposed the following pledge: “I declare that I am subject to the laws of the Republic.” Every priest who wished to exercise public worship publicly would have to adhere to this formula. One article of the proposal stipulated that this declaration was purely civil and in no way touched either doctrine or discipline. No one could doubt that this proposal would be adopted by the Council of Five Hundred and ratified by the Ancients. Thus, religious liberty was virtually restored in the Republic. It would last eight days.<br>
五百人院关于遭驱逐教士的决议，已被递交给元老院，等待将其正式确立为法律。果月7日（1797年8月24日），该决议被提上日程。负责报告的是穆莱尔（Muraire）——他曾是《教士公民组织法》的坚定拥护者之一，但如今却已彻底幻灭。他用简短的几句话强烈抨击了关于宗教活动的立法：这些法律起初只是严苛，随后变得惨无人道，最终沦为了令人发指的暴行。他提及波塔利斯（Portalis）的演讲及随后的投票，借此提醒同僚们，早在前一年，他们就曾对这些严酷措施提出过抗议。如今，必须通过明确的废除令，彻底抹除这些野蛮法令的最后痕迹。穆莱尔在结语中说道，“如果教士们有罪，那就惩罚他们，但绝不能把迫害与正义混为一谈。”报告人话音刚落，有几个人怯生生地提出延期表决，但未能引起任何共鸣；紧接着，该决议便毫无悬念地获得了批准。剩下的唯有一个问题：关于宣誓承诺的问题。在五百人院，负责宗教事务的委员会正在对此进行商议。果月10日（1797年8月27日），迪布吕埃尔（Dubruel）代表该委员会提出了以下誓词：“我声明，我服从共和国的法律。” 任何想要公开主持宗教仪式的教士，都必须接受这段话。草案中的一项条款明确规定，这项声明纯属公民世俗性质，丝毫不会干涉宗教教义或戒律。毫无疑问，这项草案必将被五百人院通过，并由元老院批准。<mark>就这样，宗教自由在共和国内实质上得到了恢复。然而，这份自由仅仅维持了八天。</mark><br>
